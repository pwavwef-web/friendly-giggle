## 📐 Platform Strategy

### LinkedIn

- **Frequency:** 1 post per week (Tuesday or Wednesday)
- **Format:** Text post (150–300 words) with a relevant image, infographic, or carousel
- **Tone:** Professional, reflective, insightful — like a thoughtful industry commentary
- **Goal:** Build a professional profile, attract recruiters, and establish authority in Ghana tourism
- **Hashtags (use 3–5 per post):**
  - `#GhanaTourism` `#UCCStudent` `#TourismManagement` `#SustainableTourism`
  - `#AfricanTourism` `#Ecotourism` `#Dissertation` `#HospitalityManagement`
  - `#FinalYearStudent` `#TourismGhana` `#VisitGhana` `#BeyondTheReturn`

### TikTok

- **Frequency:** 1 video per week (Friday or weekend)
- **Format:** 45–90 second talking-head video, voiceover slideshow, or campus/field clip
- **Tone:** Casual, energetic, educational — like talking to a friend who wants to learn
- **Goal:** Build a broad audience of students and young travellers interested in Ghana tourism
- **Content styles:**
  - 🗣️ Talking-head "Did you know?" tourism facts
  - 📖 "What I'm studying this week" course explainers
  - 🔍 "Dissertation Update" series vlogs
  - 📍 Ghana destination features (even virtual/photo-based)
  - 💡 Study tips for tourism students

## ⏱️ Time Budget — Academic-First Rule

> **Maximum content creation time: 3 hours per week total.**
> Academic work — lectures, assignments, dissertation — ALWAYS takes priority.

| Task | Time Allocation | When |
|------|----------------|------|
| LinkedIn post writing | 45 min | Tuesday evening (18:00–18:45) |
| LinkedIn post publish + engage | 15 min | Wednesday morning |
| TikTok script writing | 30 min | Friday afternoon (17:00–17:30) |
| TikTok filming/recording | 45 min | Friday evening or Saturday |
| TikTok editing + posting | 30 min | Saturday morning |
| Weekly content review | 15 min | Sunday evening |
| **Total** | **~3 hours** | Spread across the week |

**Rules for staying on track:**
1. If an assignment is due that week → skip TikTok, post LinkedIn only.
2. During CA weeks (18–22 May, 15–19 June) → pre-schedule posts or pause entirely.
3. During exam weeks (6–24 July) → content pauses completely.
4. Dissertation milestones always override content creation.
5. Batch content on quiet weeks (e.g., write 2 LinkedIn drafts at once) to build a buffer.
