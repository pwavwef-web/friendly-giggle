# LinkedIn Post Template — "Tourism Insights from a UCC Final Year Student"

> Copy this template when drafting a new LinkedIn post.
> Target length: **150–300 words**. Posts this length consistently outperform longer ones.

---

## Template

```
[HOOK — 1 sentence. Start with a bold claim, surprising fact, or a question.]

[CONTEXT — 2–3 sentences. Set up the topic. Why does it matter? What's the current situation?]

[INSIGHT — 3–5 sentences. Your academic or professional perspective. Draw from coursework, research, or Ghana tourism data. Use specific names, figures, or examples.]

[REFLECTION / LESSON — 1–2 sentences. Personal takeaway or what this means for the industry / students / professionals.]
```

## Example (Filled Template)

```
Ghana's tourism sector grew by over 1 million visitor arrivals in the three years after the Year of Return — but how much of that revenue reached local communities?

Studying Contemporary Issues in Tourism (TMG406) at UCC has pushed me to look beyond the headline numbers. The 2019 Year of Return campaign was a masterclass in diaspora marketing, attracting over 200,000 African-American visitors to Ghana. But tourism economics tells a different story about distribution.

Much of the accommodation, tours, and hospitality spending flows to large hotels and foreign-owned operators. Community-owned guesthouses, local guides, and craft vendors capture only a fraction of visitor spend. "Beyond the Return" launched follow-up initiatives, but the structural gap remains.

The lesson? Great destination marketing must be paired with inclusive value chains if tourism is to serve as a genuine poverty-reduction tool.

What's your take — is Ghana's diaspora tourism model inclusive enough?

#GhanaTourism #YearOfReturn #AfricanTourism #SustainableTourism #UCCStudent
```
