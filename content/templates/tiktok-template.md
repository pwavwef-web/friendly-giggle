# TikTok Script Template — "Tourism Insights from a UCC Final Year Student"

> Copy this template when writing a new TikTok script.
> Target video length: **45–90 seconds**. Shoot for 60 seconds as the ideal.

---

## Script Format

```
VIDEO TITLE: [Title as it will appear on screen or in the caption]
PILLAR: [Ghana Tourism Spotlight / Student Life / Dissertation Diaries / Career & Leadership / Sustainable Tourism]
WEEK: [Week number and dates]
ESTIMATED LENGTH: [e.g., 60 seconds]

---

HOOK (0–3 sec):
[Opening line spoken to camera or as text overlay. Must grab attention immediately.]
[Example: "Did you know Ghana earns over $1 billion from tourism every year?"]

---

BODY (3–50 sec):
[Main content. Break into 3–5 short points or a short story. Each point = 1–2 sentences max.]

Point 1: [...]
Point 2: [...]
Point 3: [...]

---

CALL TO ACTION (50–60 sec):
[End with a question, a "follow for more", or a challenge.]
[Example: "Comment below — have you ever visited a national park in Ghana?"]

---

CAPTION:
[Short caption for TikTok post — 1–2 sentences max + 4–6 hashtags]
[Example: Ghana's ecotourism scene is growing fast 🌿 Learn more about Kakum National Park 👇
#GhanaTourism #Ecotourism #UCCStudent #AfricanTourism #VisitGhana]

---

VISUAL NOTES:
[What to show on screen: talking head / text overlays / photos / campus shots / etc.]
```

---

## Example (Filled Script)

```
VIDEO TITLE: "Did you know? Ghana's diaspora tourism impact 🌍"
PILLAR: Ghana Tourism Spotlight
WEEK: Week 3 (12–16 May 2026)
ESTIMATED LENGTH: 60 seconds

---

HOOK (0–3 sec):
"Did you know that in 2019, over 200,000 African-Americans came to Ghana — all because of one campaign?"

---

BODY (3–50 sec):

Point 1: "That campaign was called the Year of Return — Ghana's government invited the African diaspora to come home to mark 400 years since the start of the transatlantic slave trade."

Point 2: "It was MASSIVE. Tourism arrivals jumped, international coverage exploded, and celebrities like Idris Elba and Steve Harvey made the trip."

Point 3: "Ghana earned over $3 billion from tourism that year — the highest in the country's history."

Point 4: "And it didn't stop there — 'Beyond the Return' launched programmes to keep diaspora visitors and investors coming back every year."

Point 5: "As a Tourism Management student studying Contemporary Issues, I'm researching whether this growth actually reached local communities. And the answer? It's complicated."

---

CALL TO ACTION (50–60 sec):
"Would you visit Ghana for a heritage trip? Drop 🇬🇭 in the comments if you would!"

---

CAPTION:
Ghana's Year of Return changed African tourism history 🌍 But did it benefit everyone? 
#YearOfReturn #GhanaTourism #DiasporaTourism #UCCStudent #AfricanTourism #VisitGhana

---

VISUAL NOTES:
Talking head — speak directly to camera. Use text overlays for the key stats. Can add 2–3 photos of Ghana (Cape Coast Castle, Accra streets, diaspora visitors) if available.
```
